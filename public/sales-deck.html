Objection Handling

The 3 objections you'll hear — and how to answer them.

These responses are tuned for pest control owners. Data-first answers, not fluffy reassurances.

"I already have a website."
Most common
"That's great — how is it performing? Are you getting calls from it? Most sites aren't built for local search. Ours are built specifically to rank in your city and convert visitors into calls."
Bridge: offer to pull up their current site on the call and walk through what it's missing vs. a PestFlow site side by side.
"It's too expensive."
Price objection
"At $349/mo, you need one new customer a month to break even. The average pest control customer is worth $400–$800/year. If we get you two new customers a month, you're at 5x return."
Then: "Is the question whether it will work, or whether the timing is right?" Separate budget from belief.
"I need to think about it."
Stall tactic
"What I can do is build you the site preview at no cost — 48 hours. You can look at it and then decide. Nothing to think about until you've seen exactly what you're getting. Want me to start that today?"
This converts a stall into a commitment. Most say yes. Now you have a preview appointment locked in.

Rollout in 48–72 hours, not 3 months.

On your call, position PestFlow as a fast rollout, not a multi‑month project. Walk them through exactly what happens if they say "yes" today, and then ask a single clear question.
